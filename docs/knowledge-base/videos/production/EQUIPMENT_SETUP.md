# ADSapp Video Production - Equipment Setup Guide

Complete equipment specifications and setup instructions for professional ADSapp tutorial video production.

---

## Equipment Tiers

We provide three equipment tier recommendations based on budget and production quality goals:

### Tier 1: Professional Studio (Budget: $5,000-$10,000)
**Best for**: Complete video series, maximum quality, professional production company

### Tier 2: High-Quality In-House (Budget: $1,500-$3,000)
**Best for**: Most businesses, great quality, sustainable in-house production

### Tier 3: Budget Starter (Budget: $300-$800)
**Best for**: MVP testing, proof of concept, initial videos

---

## Camera / Screen Recording

### Tier 1: Professional
- **Primary**: Blackmagic ATEM Mini Pro ($295) + Sony A7 III ($1,998)
- **Screen Capture**: OBS Studio (Free) + Elgato 4K60 Pro ($399)
- **Why**: Broadcast quality, professional switching, HDMI capture

### Tier 2: High-Quality
- **Primary**: Logitech StreamCam ($169) for talking head
- **Screen Capture**: ScreenFlow (Mac $169) or Camtasia (Win/Mac $299)
- **Why**: Excellent 1080p quality, integrated editing, affordable

### Tier 3: Budget
- **Primary**: Built-in webcam or phone camera
- **Screen Capture**: OBS Studio (Free) or QuickTime (Mac, Free)
- **Why**: Zero additional cost, decent quality, immediate start

**Recommended Settings (All Tiers)**:
- **Resolution**: 1920x1080 (1080p)
- **Frame Rate**: 60fps for screen recordings, 30fps for talking head
- **Bitrate**: 10-15 Mbps minimum
- **Format**: MP4 (H.264 codec)

## Audio Equipment

### Tier 1: Professional
- **Microphone**: Shure SM7B ($399) + Cloudlifter CL-1 ($149)
- **Interface**: Focusrite Scarlett 2i2 3rd Gen ($179)
- **Monitoring**: Sony MDR-7506 headphones ($99)
- **Treatment**: Acoustic panels ($200-$400)
- **Why**: Broadcast-quality voice, minimal post-processing needed

### Tier 2: High-Quality
- **Microphone**: Blue Yeti USB ($129) or Audio-Technica AT2020USB+ ($149)
- **Monitoring**: Audio-Technica ATH-M50x ($149)
- **Treatment**: Basic foam panels ($50-$100)
- **Why**: Excellent USB quality, plug-and-play, clear audio

### Tier 3: Budget
- **Microphone**: Built-in laptop mic + Krisp.ai noise cancellation (Free tier)
- **Monitoring**: Any headphones
- **Treatment**: Blankets/towels for dampening
- **Why**: Free, immediate start, noise cancellation helps

**Audio Recording Standards**:
- **Sample Rate**: 48kHz
- **Bit Depth**: 16-bit minimum, 24-bit preferred
- **Format**: WAV (uncompressed) for recording, MP3 320kbps for delivery
- **Levels**: Peak at -3dB to -6dB, average around -18dB
- **Noise Floor**: Below -50dB

## Lighting

### Tier 1: Professional
- **Key Light**: Aputure 300d II ($599)
- **Fill Light**: Aputure MC ($99)
- **Backlight**: Aputure MC ($99)
- **Modifiers**: Softbox ($100) + diffusion ($50)
- **Why**: Professional color accuracy, adjustable, cinematic look

### Tier 2: High-Quality
- **Key Light**: Elgato Key Light ($199)
- **Fill Light**: Neewer LED Panel ($70)
- **Natural**: Window light (Free)
- **Why**: Consistent color temp, dimmable, affordable

### Tier 3: Budget
- **Primary**: Window with sheer curtain (natural diffusion)
- **Fill**: Desk lamp with daylight bulb ($20)
- **Reflector**: White foam board ($5)
- **Why**: Free/cheap, surprisingly good results with care

**Lighting Guidelines**:
- **Color Temperature**: 5500K-5600K (daylight balanced)
- **Three-Point Setup**: Key (main), fill (soften shadows), back (separation)
- **Avoid**: Overhead fluorescent, mixed color temperatures, harsh shadows
- **Test**: Record 30-second test before each session

## Studio Setup

### Physical Space Requirements

**Minimum Space**: 8ft x 8ft (64 sq ft)
- Desk area: 4ft x 3ft
- Behind camera: 4ft clearance
- Side clearance: 2ft each side

**Ideal Space**: 12ft x 10ft (120 sq ft)
- Desk area: 5ft x 3ft
- Behind camera: 6ft clearance (better background options)
- Equipment storage: 2ft x 4ft

### Background Options

**Option 1: Clean Wall** (Free)
- Solid color wall (gray, blue, or white)
- Remove all clutter and personal items
- Hang simple artwork or brand logo

**Option 2: Blurred Background** ($0-$50)
- Use wide aperture (f/1.8-f/2.8) to blur background
- Or use software background blur
- Subtle depth, professional look

**Option 3: Brand Background** ($100-$500)
- Printed backdrop with ADSapp branding
- Roll-up banner with logo
- Professional, consistent look

**Option 4: Green Screen** ($50-$200)
- Collapsible green screen ($50-$100)
- Digital background replacement
- Most flexible, requires good lighting

### Desk Setup
- **Height**: Adjustable standing desk preferred
- **Size**: 5ft x 2.5ft minimum for monitors and equipment
- **Surface**: Non-reflective, neutral color
- **Cable Management**: Cable clips, velcro ties, desk grommet
- **Monitor Arm**: Frees desk space, adjustable positioning

## Recording Environment

### Sound Treatment

**Budget Acoustic Treatment** ($50-$200):
- **Walls**: 12x foam panels 12"x12" ($50-$100)
- **Corners**: Bass traps (DIY or $50)
- **Ceiling**: Hanging baffles (optional, $50)
- **Door**: Weather stripping to reduce outside noise ($10)

**Placement**:
- First reflection points (side walls at ear level)
- Behind and in front of recording position
- Room corners for bass absorption

**DIY Alternatives**:
- Heavy curtains or moving blankets on walls
- Bookshelf filled with books (diffusion)
- Carpet or rugs on floor
- Closet full of clothes (natural absorption)

### Noise Control

**Eliminate Common Noise Sources**:
- Turn off HVAC during recording
- Close windows (traffic, birds, lawn equipment)
- Disable notification sounds on all devices
- Silence phone and put in another room
- Turn off refrigerator compressor if nearby
- Use "Do Not Disturb" signs

**Time Recording Right**:
- Early morning (6-10 AM): Least traffic noise
- Avoid lunch hours (12-2 PM): Nearby activity
- Evening (8-10 PM): Quiet but may affect voice quality
- Weekend mornings: Ideal for extended sessions

### Environmental Factors
- **Temperature**: 68-72Â°F (comfortable for extended recording)
- **Humidity**: 30-50% (voice quality, equipment protection)
- **Ventilation**: Air circulation without noise
- **Breaks**: Step away every 30-45 minutes

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Audio Issues

**Problem**: Background hiss/noise
- **Solution**: Move further from computer, use noise gate, improve acoustic treatment

**Problem**: Echo/reverb
- **Solution**: Add more acoustic treatment, move closer to mic, use noise reduction

**Problem**: Pops and clicks
- **Solution**: Use pop filter, adjust mic technique, check cable connections

**Problem**: Volume too low
- **Solution**: Increase gain (not too much), speak closer to mic, use compression

### Video Issues

**Problem**: Dropped frames
- **Solution**: Lower recording quality, close other apps, upgrade computer

**Problem**: Out of focus
- **Solution**: Use autofocus, increase depth of field, improve lighting

**Problem**: Overexposed/underexposed
- **Solution**: Adjust lighting, use camera manual mode, add ND filter

**Problem**: Stuttering playback
- **Solution**: Render preview, reduce effects, upgrade hardware

## Recommended Starting Point

**For Most Organizations**: Start with **Tier 2** equipment.

**Why**:
- Professional enough for public-facing content
- Affordable enough to justify investment
- Room to grow without starting over
- Sustainable for ongoing production

**Upgrade Path**:
1. **Month 1-3**: Tier 2 setup, learn workflow
2. **Month 4-6**: Add better lighting if needed
3. **Month 7-12**: Upgrade audio if recording frequently
4. **Year 2+**: Consider Tier 1 for high-volume production
